# Notebook Workspace Foundation

The Notebook Workspace is where retrieved knowledge becomes personal understanding. It actively rejects the standard Markdown editor, Notion-style block editor, and traditional document editing patterns.

## Notebook Philosophy

A notebook is not a sequence of markdown blocks. It represents different forms of thinking (observations, questions, reflections, connections, reminders) that flow in a continuous, editorial manner.

Visually, the environment simulates a calm, wide-margin research journal. 

## Marginal Annotations

We prioritize the margins. Instead of highlighting text with a tooltip, annotations like "Important relationship" or "Revisit this" appear as quiet marginalia to the side of the text. This attaches the note conceptually to the thought without interrupting the reading experience.

## Subtle Connections

Knowledge references (linking back to a document, concept, or search result) act as quiet, living paths rather than standard blue hyperlinks. They are placed subordinately in the margin or subtly inline, ensuring that the thought itself remains primary.

## First-Class Uncertainty

Questions (e.g., "Why does this work?") are first-class knowledge primitives. They are styled distinctly (e.g., larger italic serif) to visually communicate uncertainty, validating the messy process of learning.

## Locus Integration

In the Notebook environment, the Locus is used to quickly record an idea with the prompt `│ Capture an idea...`. This interaction bypasses the heavy cognitive load of opening a document and formatting text.
